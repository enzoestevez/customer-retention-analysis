# Customer Retention & Revenue Concentration Analysis

End-to-end business analysis evaluating revenue dependency, customer retention dynamics, and structural risk exposure in a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace.

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis reveals that a small segment of customers generates a disproportionate share of total revenue, creating structural dependency and potential financial vulnerability.

Key insight:
- 25% of customers generate over 50% of total revenue.

This concentration highlights the strategic importance of retention, diversification, and long-term risk management.

---

## Business Context

In highly competitive e-commerce environments, sustainable growth depends on understanding:

- Who drives revenue
- How often customers return
- Where structural risks exist
- Which segments require strategic protection

This project analyzes customer behavior to support data-driven decision making at a strategic level.

---

## Key Business Questions

1. What percentage of revenue comes from repeat customers?
2. Is the company structurally dependent on a small customer segment?
3. How concentrated is revenue across categories?
4. What are the long-term strategic risks of this concentration?

---

## Data & Methodology

**Dataset:** Olist public e-commerce dataset  

**Data Modeling:**
- Merged orders, order items, products, and customers tables
- Filtered delivered orders
- Engineered revenue metrics

**Feature Engineering:**
- Orders per customer
- Revenue per customer
- Revenue concentration analysis
- Customer segmentation by purchase frequency

**Tools Used:**
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn

---

## Key Findings

- 25% of customers generate more than 50% of total revenue.
- Repeat customers disproportionately sustain business performance.
- Revenue concentration creates structural exposure.
- Financial stability depends heavily on customer retention.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

- Invest in retention and loyalty programs.
- Protect high-value customer segments.
- Diversify revenue streams to reduce dependency.
- Strengthen cross-selling and category expansion strategies.
- Continuously innovate to defend both margin and volume.

---

## Why This Project Matters

Revenue concentration is not just a financial metric — it is a structural risk indicator.

Organizations that understand revenue dependency can:
- Allocate capital more efficiently
- Protect high-value segments
- Reduce long-term exposure
- Improve strategic resilience

This project demonstrates the ability to connect data analysis with executive-level decision making.
